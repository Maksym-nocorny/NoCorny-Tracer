# NoCorny Tracer Privacy Policy

**Posted:** March 29, 2026. **Effective:** March 29, 2026.

This Privacy Policy explains how NoCorny Tracer ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses, protects, and discloses information when you use the NoCorny Tracer macOS application (the "Service"). Your privacy is our priority.

By using the Service, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this Privacy Policy. 

## 1. Information We Collect
The Service interacts with your device's hardware and local storage, but is specifically designed to operate without a centralized backend server that collects your personal data.

1.1. **User Media Data.** The Service utilizes your macOS system to record:
- Screen activity (Screen Recording)
- Video input from connected cameras (Camera)
- Audio input from connected microphones (Microphone)

1.2. **Application Metadata and Settings.** The Service stores basic application settings locally on your machine, including:
- Recording configurations (video/audio quality, layout preferences)
- User interface preferences
- Connection tokens for integrating with third-party services (e.g., Dropbox)

We **do not** collect telemetry, behavioral analytics, or usage statistics remotely.

## 2. How We Use Information
We use the collected information exclusively to provide the core functionality of the Service:
2.1. **Recording and Storage.** We use your Media Data (screen, camera, microphone) to create video and audio files locally on your device's designated temporary storage directories.
2.2. **Synchronization.** We use your authorized connection tokens to automatically securely transfer the newly created Media Data from your local machine to your connected personal cloud storage account.

## 3. Data Sharing and Third-Party Services
We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. Your data is strictly kept between your local machine and the third-party platforms you explicitly choose to connect.

3.1. **Dropbox Integration.** 
The primary sync functionality of the Service operates by uploading your recordings to your authorized Dropbox account via the Dropbox API.
- We do not access, view, or store the content of these recordings once they are transferred.
- You must authenticate with Dropbox directly via the standard Dropbox OAuth flow. The Service stores only the necessary access tokens locally on your device to maintain the connection.
- Please review the [Dropbox Privacy Policy](https://www.dropbox.com/privacy) and [Terms of Service](https://www.dropbox.com/terms) to understand how Dropbox handles the data stored on their platform.

## 4. Data Security
We implement reasonable security measures to protect the integrity of the Service and the metadata processed locally:
4.1. **Local Authorization.** System capabilities like Screen Recording, Accessibility, and Camera/Microphone access are strictly walled by macOS's native permission prompts.
4.2. **Encrypted Transfers.** Transfers to Dropbox happen securely over HTTPS (TLS encryption). 

However, no system is perfectly secure. As the Service does not retain your data on any Developer-controlled servers, the security of your stored recordings ultimately relies on your local macOS environment's security and your Dropbox account's security protocols.

## 5. Data Retention
5.1. **Local Storage.** Media Data generated by the Service is stored temporarily on your local file system during the recording and upload processes. After successful synchronization to Dropbox, we routinely clear these temporary files.
5.2. **Cloud Storage.** The final Media Data is stored permanently in your personal Dropbox account, at the path specified by the application settings. You are fully responsible for managing, retaining, or deleting these files directly via Dropbox.

## 6. User Rights and Controls
Because we do not store your data remotely:
- **Withdrawal of Consent:** You can revoke the Service’s access to your Screen, Camera, and Microphone at any time using your macOS System Settings.
- **Data Deletion:** To delete the data generated by the Service, you merely need to delete the local output files and remove the synced files from your Dropbox account.
- **Service Disconnection:** You can revoke the Service's OAuth access to your Dropbox account anytime through the linked apps section of your Dropbox account settings.

## 7. Changes to this Privacy Policy
We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of significant changes by updating the "Posted" and "Effective" dates at the top of this Policy. You are advised to review this Privacy Policy periodically for any changes.

## 8. Contact Information
If you have any questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy or our data practices, please contact us.
